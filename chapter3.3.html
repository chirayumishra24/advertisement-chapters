CHAPTER 3.3

CREATING THE CAMPAIGN (HANDMADE TO DIGITAL)
CORE PRINCIPLE

From sketch to screen — every great campaign starts with a clear visual plan and ends with a powerful digital execution.

1. THE POSTER: FROM SKETCH TO VISUAL IMPACT

GOAL IN <3 SECONDS

The poster must grab attention instantly. Your sketch should focus on a clear Visual Hierarchy.

  • The Focal Point: What is the first thing people should see? (e.g., powerful image or bold headline)
  • The Rule of Thirds: Avoid center placement. Use a 3×3 grid — place key elements at intersections for more energy.
  • Emotional Hooks – Color Triggers:
    • Blue → trust / calm
    • Red → urgency / passion
    • Green → growth / health
  • Typography: Maximum two fonts — one bold title + one clean readable font for details.

2. STORYBOARDING: THE BLUEPRINT FOR ANIMATION

WHY STORYBOARD FIRST

Before opening software, map out the “beats” of your video. A storyboard prevents getting lost in tools.

KEY FRAMES TO INCLUDE
  1. The Hook (0–3s): Visual or question that stops the scroll.
  2. The Problem: Scene showing the “pain point” your campaign addresses.
  3. The Solution: Your product/service/message in action.
  4. The Call to Action (CTA): Clear instruction (e.g., “Visit our site”, “Join the movement”).

Pro Tip: Draw small arrows to indicate motion. If text slides in from left → draw arrow pointing right.

3. DIGITAL TOOLS & EXECUTION

FROM SKETCH → DIGITAL REALITY

CANVA (GRAPHIC DESIGN & SIMPLE VIDEO)

  • Brand Kit: Set colors/fonts early → every ad stays consistent.
  • Animate Tool: Click “Animate” on elements → text fades in, images pop → static poster becomes motion graphic.

POWERPOINT (SURPRISING ANIMATION POWER)

Morph Transition cheat code: Duplicate slide → move objects → apply Morph → PowerPoint auto-animates movement.

Export as Video: File → Export → Create a Video → turns presentation into MP4 ad.
