VISUAL & VERBAL IDENTITY

Logos, Taglines & Jingles

Branding is far more than a name or a logo. It is the complete personality a product or campaign projects into the world — a blend of what people see, hear and feel when they encounter it.

1. Visual Identity — The Logo

A logo is the face of the brand — the single graphic mark people recognize instantly, even from far away or in black & white.

“The face people remember before the name.”

Key Takeaway

Great logos work across literacy levels, languages, cultures, and media — just like the iconic Pulse Polio two blue drops.

2. Verbal Identity — The Tagline

A tagline is a short, powerful sentence (usually 3–7 words) that captures the heart of the brand.

“Do Boond Zindagi Ke”
— Two drops of life
  • Simplicity wins: Easy to say, easy to remember
  • Emotional hook: Turns a medical act into an act of love & protection
  • Trust amplifier: Spoken by Amitabh Bachchan = instant credibility

3. Sound Power — The Jingle

Jingles are earworms — short melodies so catchy they live in your head forever.

  • Beat boredom: Music grabs attention when words alone are ignored
  • Subconscious memory: You hum McDonald’s “ba-da-ba-ba-ba” without effort
  • Pulse Polio example: Drums + children chanting turned vaccination days into joyful community events
 

Key Learnings — The Identity Triangle

✦
