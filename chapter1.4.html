Chapter 1.4: The Ethics (The "Safety First" Code)

In addition to creativity, professional advertising requires following a code of ethics to ensure messages are fair, truthful, and respectful.

 

1. Truthfulness vs. Fake Ads: The Veracity Rule

Advertisements must be honest and socially responsible.

  • Reflecting Reality: An advertisement should always reflect the actual features, benefits, and quality of the product or service.
  • No False Claims: You must never make false claims to lure customers. If an ad makes a promise the product cannot keep, it is considered a "fake advertisement".

Advertisements must accurately showcase real product features to avoid misleading consumers and legal issues. Exaggerated claims, like a soap promising eternal youth, erode credibility and can lead to fines under India's ASCI guidelines. Honest messaging, such as a bamboo craftsman's ad highlighting natural durability, fosters repeat business and community loyalty.

 

2. Respecting Work: Copyright and Plagiarism

When creating your own ads, you must respect the intellectual property of others.

  • Originality: Always prioritize using your own original text, images, and music.
  • Giving Credit: If you must use someone else's work, you must obtain their permission and clearly mention the source.
  • Avoiding Plagiarism: Simply copying content from the internet without credit is called plagiarism and is ethically wrong.
 

3. Cultural & Gender Sensitivity

Advertisements have a massive influence on society, so they must be designed with care.

  • Avoiding Stereotypes: Ensure your ads do not promote harmful or limiting stereotypes regarding gender, age, or background.
  • Respectful Content: Avoid any imagery or language that could be considered offensive or insensitive to different cultural groups.
 

4. Safety Protocols: Interviewing and Privacy

When working on a project to help a local business, you must follow specific safety and privacy guidelines:

  • Permission First: Always inform a parent, guardian, or teacher before visiting a business for an interview.
  • Safe Locations: Conduct your interactions in public, well-lit areas.
  • Respecting Privacy: Never take photos or record videos of a business owner or their shop without their explicit consent.
  • Data Security: Be careful not to share or upload anyone’s personal information (like phone numbers or addresses) without permission.
 

Key Learnings: The Ethical Creator

1. The Veracity Rule (Truthfulness)

The most important rule in advertising is honesty.

  • The Trust Gap: If an ad promises something the product cannot do (like a "magic" potion for height), it becomes a Fake Ad.
  • Consequences: In India, the ASCI (Advertising Standards Council of India) sets guidelines to prevent misleading claims. False ads don’t just lead to fines; they destroy consumer trust permanently.
  • The Artisan Way: For a small business hero, highlighting real quality (e.g., "Hand-stitched for durability") is more effective than exaggeration.

2. Respecting Intellectual Property

Creativity belongs to the creator. You must respect the "ownership" of ideas.

  • Copyright & Plagiarism: Copying images, music, or slogans from the internet without permission is theft.
  • The Originality Path: Always aim to create your own content. If you use external assets, ensure they are in the Public Domain or used under Fair Use with proper credit.

3. Social Responsibility & Sensitivity

Ads shape how we see the world, so they must be inclusive and kind.

  • Shattering Stereotypes: Avoid "cliché" portrayals (e.g., showing only women in the kitchen or only men as "adventurous").
  • Cultural Respect: India is diverse; an ad should never mock or offend any religion, language, or cultural group.

4. Safety and Privacy Protocols

When acting as a "Student Marketer" for local businesses, your personal safety and the owner’s privacy are the top priorities.

  • The "Permission First" Rule: Never go to an interview alone or without informing a guardian.
  • Consent is Key: Recording someone or their shop without a signed or verbal agreement is a violation of their rights.
  • Data Protection: Keep personal details (like the owner’s home address) private unless they specifically want it in the ad.
