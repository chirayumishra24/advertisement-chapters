CHAPTER 3.1

MEETING AN EXPERT & FINDING THE CLIENT

CORE PRINCIPLE

Moving from theory to real-world advertising needs a PRO + ETHICAL mindset.

Behind every brand is a REAL PERSON with a REAL BUSINESS to run.

1. MEETING AN EXPERT

WHY IT MATTERS

Learn from pros before you start creating — understand real industry standards!

  • Mentorship: Agency heads & seniors teach the real rules (clients, pricing, trends).
  • Local makers: Billboard & pamphlet creators = real execution knowledge.
  • Shopkeepers & exhibitors: Direct community pulse.
  • Business teachers: Structured strategy view.
QUESTIONS TO ASK EXPERTS
  1. What makes an ad interesting & memorable?
  2. Why spend so much money on ads? What effect on audiences? (examples!)
  3. How do you choose the medium (print, radio, TV, social…)?
  4. What problems / disadvantages do ads have?

2. FINDING THE CLIENT – MAP YOUR LOCAL SCENE

START HERE

Look at your own community first. Many small businesses have great products but no strong visual/verbal identity yet.

Look for these types
  • The Maker: Bamboo craftsman / tailor — quality & heritage focus
  • The Provider: Local baker / florist — freshness & community love
RESEARCH QUESTIONNAIRE
  1. What’s the unique story of your business? Why did you choose this one? (2–3 reasons)
  2. Who is your typical customer? (age, location, needs)
  3. What feeling do you want when people hear your name?
  4. Who are your main local competitors?

3. THE PARTNERSHIP – ETHICS & CONTRACTS

CORE IDEA

Professionalism = clear communication + safety. You’re not doing a favor — you’re entering a REAL partnership.

Pitch Like a Pro

“I want to help your business reach more people with a consistent visual identity and emotional hook that builds trust.”

WHY CONTRACTS MATTER

A contract protects both sides — turns you from “student” into “consultant”.

SIMPLE CONTRACT TEMPLATE

I, (owner) of agree to work with Grade 8 students from at to create an advertisement for my business.

We agree on:

  • Students can photograph product/owner with approval.
  • Mutually agreed dates & times for info sharing.
  • Regular progress updates to owner.
  • All interaction in presence of teacher/parent.

AD-MAN SAYS:

Before contracts existed, many early designers got ZERO payment after doing full work! A contract is your INVISIBLE SHIELD — turn “student favor” into REAL pro protection!

KEY LEARNINGS

RESEARCH FIRST

No info = no good design. Know your baker before the logo!

LOCAL = POWER

Small stories (bamboo craftsman) = strongest emotional hooks today.

CONTRACT = SUPERPOWER

Turns “student favor” into real pro work. Protects everyone.
